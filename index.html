      About nirviral.com

      What is nirviral.com?

      nirviral.com is a free, in-browser mp4 player that can be used to view movies and TV shows on the internet at insane speeds compared to traditional methods.

      How it works

      It works by using peer-to-peer technology to stream video files from a network of other users - as opposed to a central server. This is known as the BitTorrent protocol and is a safe and widely used way of sharing files. More info »

      Is it legal?

      nirviral.com is primarily a front-end for a torrent client, which in essence is legal. Nirviral.com does not host, store, archive, index, or distribute any media. However, using this technology to share copyrighted materials is not legal in every country. For this reason, you must check that copyright law in your country permits you to use this service before you continue. For more information on the legality of file sharing in your country, click here.

      Which browsers are supported?

      nirviral.com is currently only supported in the latest versions of Google Chrome and Firefox on Windows and Mac. This limitation is due to the Torque plugin's limited browser support. If support is added to Torque, it will be added here.

      Where does nirviral.com get its data?

      There is no data stored on the nirviral.com server other than the files required to run the application - all other data is pulled from other services. Torrents for movies are indexed by YIFY Torrents and torrents for TV shows are indexed by Kickass Torrents. This data is then combined with information from TMDB to produce a clean and simple interface.

      Is this in any way affiliated with Popcorn Time or Time4Popcorn?

      No. This application was written completely from scratch to provide the best possible web-based torrent streaming service.

      What is Torque?

      Torque is a completely safe and legal plugin, developed by BitTorrent. It is an interface-less torrent client that runs in the background alongside your browser to provide torrent technology to nirviral.com.

      Why am I being told that I need to install VLC media player?

      Certain TV shows are encoded in a way that means they can't be played natively by your browser. Because of this, any files that are in the .avi format will require the VLC player to be installed before they can be streamed on the web.
